Why this memorial exists

A Note from Dan Schacher

Many of the photos and videos here were recorded at John’s wake in October 2025, where friends honored him by sharing memories and raising shots of Machine Head’s Shotgun Blast Whiskey — a limited-edition collector’s case that I had gifted to John before what unknowingly was our final concert together.

The Shotgun Blast whiskey held special meaning to John & me as it represented so many of the things he loved — whiskey, metal music, hunting with shotguns with his family & friends, and the iconic glowing briefcase from Pulp Fiction, one of our favorite movies growing up.

Our final concert together after nearly 40 years of shows was Machine Head in Chicago in 2025, where we even spent time backstage with the band before & after the show. Those memories — along with the photos & videos you see here — are some of my favorites from our lifelong friendship.

I created this memorial site not only to honor John today, but also as a future gift for his son, Johnny (JB3). When Johnny is older, this collection of stories, photos, and videos will help him see the many sides of his father through the memories of the people who knew and loved him.

Until that day, we can all continue to gather, share & preserve these memories so Johnny can one day experience the incredible life, friendships, and impact of his dad, the legendary "Polish Prince" John Nicholas Bielski II.

— Dan Schacher

Sign in to Google if needed

If Google asks you to sign in, use the email address to which Dan gave you editor access. Once signed in, you should see the shared family folder.

3

Open the folder where you want to add memories

Inside the shared drive folder, open the main photo or video folder. You can also open any subfolder if the family organizes photos by year, event, or topic.

4

Upload your own photos and videos

In Google Drive, click New, then choose File upload or Folder upload. You can also drag and drop photos and videos directly into the open folder window.

5

Name files clearly

Consider renaming files with a date, event, or short description so family members know what they are viewing, such as 1998_family_trip.jpg or birthday_video_2007.mp4.

6

Come back to the memorial page anytime

The slideshow on this page uses curated site photos, while the family album remains the shared place for everyone to view and contribute the full photo and video collection.

Need help? If the folder does not open or uploading is blocked, contact Dan Schacher so he can confirm that your Google account has editor permission.
